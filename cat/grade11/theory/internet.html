Internet, IoT & 4IR Grade 11

Grade 11 extends Internet knowledge with VoIP, online services, IoT, the Fourth Industrial Revolution, VR/AR, social networks and mobile Internet access. Also covers web usability and email management.

Types of Digital Communication

VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol)

VoIP transmits voice calls over the Internet instead of traditional telephone lines. Examples: WhatsApp calls, Skype, Zoom.

AdvantagesDisadvantages
Much cheaper than traditional calls (especially international)Requires stable Internet connection
Can call anywhere in the world for free or low costCall quality depends on bandwidth
Integrates with other Internet services (video, messaging)Cannot make emergency calls if Internet is down

Video Conferencing

Allows multiple people to see and hear each other in real time over the Internet (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet). Used for remote work, online lessons, doctor consultations.

Online Services

Online banking
Manage accounts, transfer money, pay bills via bank website or app
Online shopping (e-commerce)
Browse and buy products online (Takealot, Amazon); pay via card or EFT
Online booking
Book flights, hotels, concerts, restaurant reservations
Online government services
Renew ID/passport, pay traffic fines, apply for tertiary (NSFAS)

Internet of Things (IoT)

DEFINITION

IoT (Internet of Things) = everyday physical devices connected to the Internet that can collect, send and receive data automatically — without human intervention.

How IoT works:

Sensing (Input)
→
Processing (AI/Analysis)
→
Output (Action/Alert)

IoT Examples

SectorDeviceWhat it does

AgricultureLivestock monitorsGPS collars track animals; health sensors detect illness

Technologies involved in IoT

  • Big data: IoT devices generate massive amounts of data over time
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): analyses the data to make decisions
  • Cloud computing: stores and processes the data remotely
  • 5G/Wi-Fi/Bluetooth: connects the devices to the network

Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR)

DEFINITION

The Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) is the current era of rapid technological change characterised by the fusion of digital, physical and biological systems. Key technologies: AI, robotics, IoT, 3D printing, biotechnology, blockchain.

VR and AR

Virtual Reality (VR)Augmented Reality (AR)
DefinitionCompletely replaces reality with a simulated digital environmentOverlays digital information onto the real-world view
Device neededVR headset (e.g. Meta Quest, PlayStation VR)Smartphone, smart glasses (no special device needed)
ExamplesVR gaming, virtual property tours, training simulationsPokémon GO, Snapchat-filters, IKEA Place-app

Other 4IR Technologies

Autonomous vehicles
Self-driving cars — vehicles that use sensors, AI and networks to make informed decisions on the road and reach destinations safely
Drones
An unmanned aircraft / flying robot controlled remotely or flying autonomously via software flight plans, on-board sensors and GPS
3D printing
Prints physical objects layer by layer; used for prototypes, medical equipment/limbs and housing
Virtual community
A group of people who gather online to share ideas and interests
EXAM TIP — 4IR & jobs

A common "discuss" question: how will 4IR/automation affect work? Give both sides — it destroys some routine/manual jobs (replaced by robots/AI), but creates new jobs (programmers, data analysts, robot technicians) and requires people to re-skill.

Social Networks and Mobile Internet

Social Network Websites

  • Platforms for sharing content, connecting with others: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, TikTok, LinkedIn
  • Advantages: stay connected, share information quickly, networking opportunities
  • Disadvantages: cyberbullying, fake news, privacy risks, addiction, time-wasting

Portable and Mobile Internet Access

Wi-Fi Hotspot (personal)
Use your phone as a router (tethering) — shares your mobile data via Wi-Fi to other devices
Wi-Fi Hotspot (public)
Free or paid Wi-Fi in public places (airports, coffee shops, libraries)
Bluetooth tethering
Share phone's internet via Bluetooth (slower than Wi-Fi hotspot)
4G / 5G mobile data
Cellular data via SIM card; 5G is much faster with lower latency

Web Usability — Evaluating Websites

A usable website is easy to navigate and use. Evaluate websites against these criteria:

CriterionWhat to look for
ReadabilityClear font, appropriate size, good contrast between text and background
NavigationEasy to find links; clear menus; logical structure; search function
ConsistencySame colour scheme, fonts and layout on all pages
LayoutOrganised; important information visible without scrolling; balanced text and images
TypographyAppropriate font choice; not too many different fonts; headings clearly different from body text
