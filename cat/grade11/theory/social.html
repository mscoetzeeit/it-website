Social Implications Grade 11

Grade 11 social implications covers network ethics, social engineering, malware, AI and academic integrity, health risks, privacy and data protection.

Ethical Use of Networks

  • Only use the network for authorised purposes
  • Do not access other users' files without permission
  • Do not use the network to distribute illegal software or content
  • Report suspected security breaches immediately
  • Follow the organisation's Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

Unauthorised Access

Unauthorised access = accessing a computer, network or account without permission. This includes:

  • Guessing or stealing someone's password
  • Hacking into a network or system
  • Using someone else's login credentials

Authentication methods to prevent unauthorised access:

Username + Password
Most basic — password should be strong and unique for each account
Multi-step verification
Requires a second proof (OTP via SMS, authenticator app) after password
Biometrics
Fingerprint, face or iris scan — unique to each person
Smart cards / access tags
Physical card with embedded chip; tap to gain access
CAPTCHA
A test (e.g. distorted text or 'select all traffic lights' images) that distinguishes a real human from an automated bot, to stop programs abusing forms

Social Engineering

DEFINITION

Social engineering = manipulating people psychologically to trick them into giving up confidential information or performing actions that compromise security. It exploits human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Types of Social Engineering / Deception Attacks

Phishing
Fake email that appears to be from a trusted source (bank, government) — tricks you into clicking a link or entering personal info on a fake website
Spear phishing
Targeted phishing attack using your personal details to seem more convincing
Pharming
Redirects you from a legitimate website to a fake one even if you type the correct URL (DNS poisoning)
Vishing
Voice phishing — fraudulent phone calls pretending to be banks/tech support
Pretexting
Creating a false scenario ("I'm from IT, I need your password to fix an issue")

Malware — Types and Infection Methods

Malware = malicious software designed to damage, disrupt or gain unauthorised access to systems.

TypeWhat it does
VirusAttaches to legitimate files; spreads when files are shared; can corrupt or delete data
WormSelf-replicating; spreads through networks without user action; slows networks
Trojan horseDisguised as legitimate software; once installed, creates a backdoor for attackers
RansomwareEncrypts your files; demands payment (ransom) to unlock them
SpywareSecretly monitors your activity and sends information to the attacker
AdwareDisplays unwanted advertisements; often bundled with free software
KeyloggerRecords every keystroke — used to steal passwords and credit card numbers

Common Infection Methods

  • Email attachments with malicious files
  • Clicking infected links in emails or websites
  • Downloading software from untrustworthy sources
  • Plugging in an infected USB drive
  • Peer-to-peer (P2P) file sharing networks
  • Visiting compromised websites (drive-by download)

Protecting Yourself Online

  • Install and keep updated antivirus/anti-malware software
  • Enable multi-step verification on all important accounts
  • Use SSL/HTTPS websites for banking and shopping (look for padlock)
  • Authorise app permissions carefully — only grant what is necessary
  • Back up data regularly (3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite)
  • Do not click unknown links or open unexpected attachments

Data Protection and Privacy

Information accuracy
Data should be correct and up to date. Inaccurate data leads to wrong decisions (GIGO principle: Garbage In, Garbage Out)
Data protection
Keeping data safe from loss, theft or corruption; includes backups, access controls, encryption
Verification
Checking that data entered is consistent and correct (e.g. re-enter password to confirm)
Validation
Checking that data entered is within acceptable range/format (e.g. date must be between 1/1/2000 and today)

E-Commerce and E-Banking

Buying something online or paying a bill through a banking app saves a trip to a shop or branch — but it also means trusting your money and personal details to a screen instead of a person.

E-commerce
Buying and selling goods/services online (e.g. Takealot). Advantages: shop 24/7, compare prices easily, no travel needed. Risks: can't inspect the item first, possible fake/scam stores
E-banking
Doing banking online or via an app (transfers, paying bills, checking balances). Advantages: no queues, available anytime. Risks: phishing scams, needs a secure internet connection
SIGNS OF A SECURE ONLINE STORE/BANK

Look for https:// (not just http:// ) and a padlock icon in the address bar before entering card details — this means the connection is encrypted.

AI — Ethical Use and Academic Integrity

Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini) can generate text, code, images and more. Ethical use means:

  • Using AI as a tool to help — not to do all the thinking for you
  • Academic integrity: submitting AI-generated work as your own is plagiarism
  • Always credit AI assistance in your work
  • Verify AI outputs — AI can produce incorrect information (hallucinations)
  • Consider data privacy — do not enter personal/confidential information into AI tools
BIG DATA

Big data refers to extremely large datasets that cannot be processed by traditional methods. Characteristics: Volume (huge amount), Velocity (generated very fast), Variety (many types). Databases are essential for storing and querying big data. AI uses big data to identify patterns and make predictions.

Health Risks and Power Issues

Factors affecting health
Screen time, posture, RSI, eye strain, radiation, noise; all worsen with poor ergonomic setup
Power settings/saving
Set screen brightness low; use sleep/hibernate mode; reduces electricity consumption and extends battery life
UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
Battery backup device — keeps computer running briefly during power cut so you can save work and shut down safely
Power surge protection
Surge protector strips prevent voltage spikes from damaging equipment
EXAM TIP — Malware Questions

Common question: "Name and describe ONE type of malware." Know the definition AND what each type does: Virus (spreads via files), Worm (spreads via network), Trojan (disguised), Ransomware (encrypts + demands payment), Spyware (secretly monitors). Also know infection methods and how to protect against them.
