Presentations Grade 10 · PAT only

Presentations are taught in Term 3 as part of PAT Phase 1. They are used to present your Information Management findings. Presentations are assessed through the PAT, NOT in the June or end-of-year P1 exam.

What is a Presentation?

A presentation is a slideshow made up of individual slides, each containing text, images and other objects. PowerPoint (.pptx) is the standard format used in CAT.

The PowerPoint Interface

Slides panel
Left side — shows thumbnail previews of all slides
Slide area
Main editing area — design your current slide here
Notes panel
Below slide area — add speaker notes (not shown during slideshow)
Ribbon
Home, Insert, Design, Transitions, Animations, Slide Show, Review, View

Rules for Good Presentations

  • Keep it simple — use keywords, not full sentences
  • Consistent design — use the same theme/colour scheme throughout
  • Limit content — 6×6 rule: max 6 bullet points per slide, 6 words per point
  • Use visuals — images, charts and diagrams are more engaging than plain text
  • Readable fonts — minimum size 24pt for body, 36pt+ for headings
  • High contrast — dark text on light background or vice versa
  • Avoid reading directly from the slides — they support, not replace, your presentation

Applying Designs

  • Themes: Design tab → Themes gallery — choose a built-in design (colours, fonts, background)
  • Design Ideas: PowerPoint suggests layouts automatically (Design tab → Design Ideas)
  • Slide size: Design → Slide Size → Standard (4:3) or Widescreen (16:9)

View Options

Normal
Default editing view — slide + notes panel
Slide Sorter
Shows all slides as thumbnails; useful for rearranging
Notes Page
Full-page view of slide + notes — print for presenter
Reading View
Plays the slideshow in a window (not full screen)
Slide Show
Full-screen presentation (F5 from beginning; Shift+F5 from current)

Custom Animations (Basic)

Animations make individual objects on a slide appear, move or disappear.

  • Select object → Animations tab → choose an animation (Appear, Fly In, Fade, etc.)
  • Set Start: On Click, With Previous, or After Previous
  • Animation Pane: Animations → Animation Pane — lists all animations in order; drag to reorder

Slide Transitions (Basic)

Transitions are effects between slides when moving from one to the next.

  • Transitions tab → choose an effect (Fade, Push, Wipe, etc.)
  • Set speed and whether to advance On Click or After a number of seconds
  • Apply to All to use the same transition throughout

Hyperlinks in Presentations

Add a hyperlink to a slide object: select text or object → Insert → Link → choose URL, file, email or another slide in the presentation.

Adding Multimedia

  • Video: Insert → Video → This Device or Online Video
  • Audio / Voice recording: Insert → Audio → Record Audio
  • Images: Insert → Pictures; resize using corner handles

Integration with Other Applications

  • Copy a chart from Excel → paste into a PowerPoint slide
  • Paste text from Word with formatting preserved
  • Embed or link objects from other Office applications
PAT TIP

Your PAT presentation summarises your research findings. Include: title slide, introduction, findings (with charts/graphs from Excel), conclusions, recommendations and a bibliography. Use charts and images to make it visual and engaging.
