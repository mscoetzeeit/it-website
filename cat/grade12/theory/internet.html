Internet & E-Communication Grade 12

Grade 12 extends internet knowledge: video conferencing, advanced browser features, blogs/vlogs/podcasts, GPS and geo-tagging, social networks, mobile devices as communication tools.

Video Conferencing

Video conferencing allows multiple people to see and hear each other simultaneously using Internet connections (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet).

AdvantagesDisadvantages
No travel required — saves time and moneyRequires reliable internet; poor connection = poor quality
Connect globally in real timeLack of physical presence — harder to read body language
Record meetings for later reviewTechnical problems (audio/video failures)
Share screens and collaborate on documentsSecurity risks — "Zoom bombing" (uninvited guests)

Good Practices for Video Conferencing

  • Test audio and video before the meeting starts
  • Mute when not speaking to reduce background noise
  • Use a professional background or virtual background
  • Look into the camera, not at your own image
  • Use waiting rooms and passwords to prevent uninvited guests
  • Use the hand-raise feature rather than interrupting

Web Browser Features — Advanced

Bookmarks / Favourites
Save frequently visited URLs for quick access; organised in folders
History
List of recently visited websites; useful for finding a page you forgot to bookmark; can be cleared for privacy
Home page settings
Set a specific page (or blank page) to open when the browser starts or when you click the home button
Blocking websites
Parents/schools can block specific URLs or categories of sites (parental controls, content filtering)
Caching
Browser saves copies of web pages/images locally so they load faster on repeat visits; can cause issues with outdated content (Ctrl+Shift+Delete to clear)
Browser extensions
Add-ons that extend browser functionality; installed from the browser's extension store
Private browsing
Incognito (Chrome) / InPrivate (Edge) — browsing history, cookies and form data NOT saved on the device; still visible to ISP and websites

Uses of Computer Communications

Blog
Online journal/website with regular text posts on a topic; readers can comment; author maintains it (WordPress, Blogger)
Vlog
Video blog — same as a blog but in video format (YouTube, TikTok)
Podcast
Audio content (like a radio show) available for download/streaming; subscribe to new episodes (Spotify, Apple Podcasts)
Vodcast
Video podcast — same concept as podcast but with video
Wiki
Collaborative web page that anyone can edit (Wikipedia); community maintains accuracy

GPS and Geo-Tagging

GPS (Global Positioning System)
Uses satellites to determine exact location (latitude and longitude) anywhere on Earth. Used in: navigation apps, fleet tracking, fitness trackers, emergency services.
Geo-tagging
Embedding GPS coordinates into a file (photo, social media post, tweet). When you take a photo on a phone with location services on, the exact coordinates are saved in the image metadata.
PRIVACY RISK — Geo-tagging

Sharing a geo-tagged photo online reveals exactly WHERE you were. This can be exploited by stalkers, burglars (knowing you're not home) or others with bad intentions. Always check location settings before sharing photos publicly.

EXAM TIP — Private Browsing

A common misconception: private browsing is NOT fully anonymous. Your ISP, employer, school network administrator and the websites you visit can still see what you're doing. It only prevents your local device from saving history, cookies and form data. Use a VPN for actual privacy from your ISP.

Common Exam Mistakes

Incognito / private browsing makes you anonymous online.
It only stops your device saving history; your ISP and the websites still see you.
Downloading and streaming are the same.
Downloading saves a full copy; streaming plays in real time without saving.
A bookmark and the browser history are the same.
A bookmark is a link you deliberately saved; history is an automatic record of sites you visited.
