Social Implications Grade 12

Grade 12 social implications focuses on computer crime, malware safeguards, the impact of social networking and technology on society, and making informed recommendations in real-world scenarios.

Computer Crime

CrimeDescription
Hardware theftPhysical stealing of computers/devices
Software theft/piracyIllegal copying or using software without a licence
Information/data theftStealing confidential data (customer records, trade secrets)
Identity theftStealing personal info to impersonate someone (open accounts, commit fraud)
Bandwidth theftUsing someone's internet connection without permission
Theft of time/servicesUsing work computers/internet for personal use during work hours

Internet-Based Crimes

Fraud / Scams
Tricking people into giving money or info (419 scams, fake lottery wins, romance scams)
Phishing
Fake emails/sites tricking you into revealing passwords or banking details
DDoS-Attack
Distributed Denial of Service — flooding a server with traffic from many computers to crash it
Bots and Zombies
Malware turns computers into "zombies" controlled remotely; a "botnet" of zombies is used for DDoS attacks and spam
Hacking / Cracking
Gaining unauthorised access to systems; cracking = malicious hacking
Misuse of personal information
Collecting/selling personal data without consent (violates POPIA)

Malware — Safeguards

Types of malware (revise from Grade 11): Virus, Worm, Trojan, Ransomware, Spyware, Adware, Keylogger.

Warning Signs of Infection

  • Computer suddenly very slow
  • Frequent crashes or freezing
  • Unexpected pop-up ads
  • Unknown programs running or installed
  • Files missing, renamed or encrypted
  • Browser homepage changed without permission
  • Excessive network/data activity

Safeguards Against Threats

SafeguardHow it protects you
Anti-malware softwareDetects and removes viruses, spyware, etc. — keep it UPDATED
FirewallBlocks unauthorised network connections
Access levels / permissionsLimits who can access what data
AuthenticationUsername + password, biometrics, two-step verification
Automatic software updatesPatches security vulnerabilities promptly
Check URL of linksHover to verify before clicking; look for HTTPS and padlock
Check spelling/tone of messagesPhishing emails often have errors and create false urgency
SSL / HTTPSEncrypts data between browser and website
Privacy settingsLimit who can see your information on social media
Regular backupsRecover data if hit by ransomware (3-2-1 rule)

Impact of Social Networking and Technology

Cyber profile / Digital footprint
The accumulated record of your online activity; can affect job prospects and reputation; very difficult to erase
Crowdfunding
Raising money from many people online for a project or cause (e.g. BackaBuddy, GoFundMe)
Fake news / Misinformation
False information spread (deliberately = disinformation; accidentally = misinformation); spreads fast on social media
Information overload
Being overwhelmed by too much information, making it hard to make decisions or focus
Distributed computing power
Combining the processing power of many computers globally to tackle huge problems (research, blockchain)

Legal and Ethical Issues

Confidentiality / Privacy
Keeping personal/sensitive information protected; protected by POPIA in SA
Copyright
Legal protection of original works; using without permission is infringement
Plagiarism
Presenting someone else's work as your own; includes copying from internet/AI without crediting
Intellectual property
Creations of the mind (inventions, designs, software) protected by law
Identity theft
Stealing personal info to impersonate someone for financial gain
Online harassment
Defamation (false damaging statements) and cyberbullying via digital platforms

Remote Access and the Changing Workplace

VPN (Virtual Private Network)
Secure encrypted connection to access a private network remotely; enables safe remote work
E-commuting / Remote work
Working from home/anywhere via the internet instead of travelling to an office
E-learning
Online education — courses, lectures and assessments delivered digitally
Gig economy
Short-term, flexible jobs via platforms (Uber, freelancing); decentralised labour
Benefits of remote workDrawbacks of remote work
No commute — saves time and moneyIsolation; less team collaboration
Flexible hours and locationHarder to separate work and home life
Wider talent pool for employersRequires reliable internet and equipment
Lower office costsSecurity risks accessing company data remotely

EXAM TIP — Scenario Questions (Question 9 & 10)

Grade 12 Paper 2 ends with integrated scenario questions worth ±25 marks each. These require you to apply knowledge to a real-world situation and make informed recommendations. Structure: identify the issue → explain it → recommend a solution → justify your recommendation. Use specific terminology and link directly to the scenario details. Paragraph answers with reasons earn the most marks.
