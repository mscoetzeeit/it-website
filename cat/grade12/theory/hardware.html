Hardware & Buying Decisions Grade 12

Grade 12 consolidates all hardware knowledge and focuses on making informed buying decisions — choosing the right hardware for specific users and scenarios, interpreting specifications, and troubleshooting.

Making Buying Decisions — What to Consider

Processor (CPU)

  • Speed: measured in GHz — higher = faster processing
  • Cores: more cores = better multitasking (quad-core, hexa-core, octa-core)
  • Generation: newer generations are more efficient (e.g. Intel Core i7 12th gen vs 10th gen)
  • Match to task: basic tasks = Core i3/Ryzen 3; heavy tasks = Core i7/i9 or Ryzen 7/9

RAM

  • 8 GB — adequate for basic home/office use
  • 16 GB — recommended for multitasking, software development
  • 32 GB+ — video editing, 3D rendering, heavy data work
  • More RAM = more programs open simultaneously without slowing down

  • Dedicated GPU: needed for gaming, video editing, 3D design, machine learning; has its own VRAM

Monitors and Screens

  • Resolution: 1920×1080 (Full HD) standard; 2560×1440 (QHD); 3840×2160 (4K)
  • Size: 24" standard office; 27"+ for design; 32"+ for power users
  • Aspect ratio: 16:9 widescreen is standard; ultrawide 21:9 for multi-tasking

Interpreting Hardware Specifications

Sample advert: "Intel Core i7-13700H, 16 GB DDR5 RAM, 512 GB NVMe SSD, NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 8 GB, 15.6" FHD IPS, Windows 11"

SpecWhat it means
Intel Core i7-13700HHigh-performance CPU, 13th generation, H = high performance (laptop)
16 GB DDR5 RAM16 gigabytes of fast DDR5 RAM; good for multitasking
512 GB NVMe SSD512 gigabytes of very fast solid state storage
NVIDIA RTX 4060 8 GBDedicated GPU with 8 GB VRAM; good for gaming and video editing
15.6" FHD IPS15.6 inch screen; Full HD (1920×1080); IPS panel for good colour accuracy

Productivity, Efficiency and Accessibility

  • Productivity: how much work can be done in a given time
  • Efficiency: completing tasks correctly with minimum wasted effort/resources
  • Accuracy: reduced human errors through automation and validation
  • Accessibility: ensuring technology is usable by people with disabilities

EXAM TIP — "Recommend Hardware for a User" Questions

Structure your answer: (1) Name the device/component, (2) Give the specification, (3) Justify why it's suitable. Example: "Recommend a 16 GB RAM because the power user runs multiple applications simultaneously and needs enough memory to prevent slowdowns." Always link your recommendation to the user's needs described in the scenario.
